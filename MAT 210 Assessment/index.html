Alex the geologist is in the desert, 6 km from a long straight road. Alex's jeep travels at 48 kph on the road and 20 kph across the desert. There is a supply depot on the road, 16 km from the nearest point N on the road to Alex's position.

  1. Let x be the distance (in km) along the road from N to the point where Alex joins the road. Write an expression for the total travel time, in hours. Include all terms; you do not need to simplify fully.
  2. Compute the time if Alex drives straight to N then along the road and the time driving entirely through the desert to the depot.
  3. Alex claims there is a value between 0 and 16 that gives a shorter total time. Without solving for the exact minimum, give a convincing mathematical argument that such a value must exist.
  4. Pose one meaningful follow-up question this problem makes you wonder about.
